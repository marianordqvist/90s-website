My week of TV shows in 1997

LET'S GO!

MONDAY

18.00

BJÖRNES MAGASIN

Aah, we are off to a great start! There is in my humble opinion no safer place than Björnes Magasin. This show was started in the 80s but ran all the way until 2004, and it was about a bear called Björne, and his friends Snigel (a snail), Hugo (a wooden dog), Branka (a hobby horse), Lisa (a flower) and Bertil (a palm tree). Björne loves to eat gingerbread cookies and watches short movies with his friends.

Björne also had a lot of human friends that came to visit: Anders Lundin, Carl-Einar Häckner, Vanna Rosenberg, Anders Linder, Roger Westberg and Johan Ulveson to mention a few. How Björne has come to know so many famous people is not explained though.

HJÄRNKONTORET

Tuesday - maybe the worst day of the week? Mondays are close to the weekend so it is still okay to chill a little, but when tuesday rolls around it is time to get serious.

That is probably why we got to watch Hjärnkontoret (translates to something like "The brain office") on this tuesday evening. In this show Victoria Dyring teaches us about science and show us different kinds of cool experiments. Yey!

ALFONS ÅBERG

Alfons Åberg (called Alfie Atkinsin english) is a little boy who lives in Sweden with his dad. He has an imaginary friend named Mållgan, and a white cat named Pussel.

The program is about different everyday events that most children can relate to, like wanting to be as big as ones older cousins or feeling scared at night. Alfie and his dad love each other very much, and there is no mention of a mom or wife.

LILLA AKTUELLT

Now we are officially on the weekend-side of the week - but - we are still not quite there. So it is time to be a little serious again. Today we get to watch Lilla Aktuellt, which is a news program for children.

It premiered on September 15 1993 and is still running today. Lilla Aktuellt is Sweden's only news program for children.

Lilla Aktuellt has won several awards, for example Barncancerfondens Journalistpris (Children's Cancer Foundation Journalism Prize)

FRIDAY

18.30

MUMINTROLLET

Mumin is a friendly, adventurous, and curious momin troll. However, he is also anxious and don't want to venture out on adventures alone. He is well-mannered and polite but somewhat naive. He lives his life finding wonder in simple things like picking beautiful stones or seashells. He lives with his mom and dad in the mumin-house, and during the winter they go into hibernation.

Mumins friends are Snorkfröken, Sniff, Snusmumriken and Lilla My.

SATURDAY

18.30

5 MYROR ÄR FLER ÄN 4 ELEFANTER

Fem myror är fler än fyra elefanter is a Swedish children's program that aired in the 70s first. Each episode had a theme based on a specific letter and number. Magnus, Brasse, and Eva played consistent roles throughout the series: a pedantic character, a messy character, and an orderly character.

The title, "Fem myror är fler än fyra elefanter," translates to "Five ants are more than four elephants" and emphasizes the concept of quantity and size comparison for children.

SUNDAY

18.30

Skrotnisse och hans vänner

To be a little honest - This is the only show that I have problems recalling. Probably because it looks horrifying! Anyhow, Wikipedia has informed me: Scrap dealer Nisse and his son Kalle run a junkyard in a small town (loosely based on Trollhättan). Nisses childhood friend, the genius Bertil Enstöring is in the process of writing a book about all his inventions and technological wonders. The malicious city engineer Ture Björkman decides to seize Bertil's book by any means necessary.

The series revolves around how Nisse and Kalle help Bertil avoid Ture. Bertil eventually realizes that he can't live in the town anymore and is transported in a box to the Sahara Desert to escape.
